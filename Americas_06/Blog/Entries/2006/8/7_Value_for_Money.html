Sunday, 6 August 2006

 

With the ridiculous price of bananas back home I thought I’d sample a couple of California’s finest for breakfast. Well I’ve done the sums and it seems better value to fly to California and eat them than to pick up a hand at Woolies. Ok a little exaggeration...


Again another fabulous San Francisco day. I noticed in the mirror this morning that due to having a blonde moment yesterday and forgetting to wear sunscreen my face now resembles the colour of the Golden Gate Bridge. Ouch.. Flick if you read this bring Aloe Vera!!


My morning took me aboard a SF electric bus to Alamo Square where I sat and had a nice breakfast overlooking the city skyline and the famous Painted Ladies. I hadn’t caught public transport in years so it was a bit of a learning experience. I jumped off at the wrong stop and photographed what I thought was the famous San Francisco landmark. Whilst sitting down enjoying my Walgreens breakfast I noticed a billion (again a little exaggeration) tour buses going straight past what I thought was the infamous San Francisco Victorian houses. Here I am thinking that those suckers don’t even have the pleasure of stepping off the bus for a photograph. A bit stiff for the $US28 they paid. Anyway after finishing up my meal I thought I’d walk through the park and see what else was around. A two minute stroll through a couple of paths and over a mound I found the real ladies.... and about 100,000 Japanese Tourists (no exaggeration).


This morning I also wandered down and registered for the WWDC. I woke up early so I decided to get down there first thing. Being a tourist I took a couple of photo’s. Place looks wicked, can’t wait for the Keynote address tomorrow from my mate Steve.
