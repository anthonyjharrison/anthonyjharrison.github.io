Friday, 8 September 2006

 

...but no chair or people with games!


Yesterday, after a sad goodbye to Steph and James we arrived safely in Calgary - home of the 1988 winter Olympics. We didn’t hang around long to take it in as we had a date with the magnificent Canadian Rockies! Armed with our gas guzzling SUV (true American style it is super fuel efficient running at 1 mile to the gallon!) we took off to explore Lake Louise and Moraine Lake. The water colour was amazing. The bright turquoise hue is evident due to the water’s journey from the glaciers above. Its not all that warm either - around 10 degrees.


Our drive this morning was only 5 minutes old before we had to pull over. No major emergency, the road was blocked ahead by a mass of tourists. We slowed and peered into the bushes to see what the fuss was about. Low and behold it was a cute (I can say cute because we were at a safe distance locked in our car) grizzly bear. In true Yogi Bear style he most certainly wasn’t camera shy.


Our travels took us back to Lake Louise where we did a lovely walk around the lake in the sunshine. The lake is truly magnificent and quite popular to many walks of life - luckily no bears! Lunchtime led us into British Colombia and Yoho National Park to see the Takakkaw Falls (one of the tallest falls in Canada). It was no where near as wide as Niagara, but we could feel the spray as we approached the bottom of the falls.


Our afternoon took us back to Banff for a not so refreshing dip in the 40 degree water of the natural thermal springs! We’ll have to remember to do this in the winter!
